SAT and ACT Information

SAT

About the Test
The SAT is multiple choice, pencil-and-paper entrance exam used by colleges and universities to compare applicants on a common point. It is administered by the College Board, a not-for-profit organization that provides college readiness programs and services to more than seven million students.

Quick Facts
Sections: Math, Evidence-Based Reading and Writing, Essay (optional)
Time: 3 hours, 3 hours 50 minutes with essay
Cost: $46, $60 with essay
Highest Possible Score: 1600

Scoring the SAT
The Math and Evidence-Based Reading and Writing sections are scored on a scale of 200 to 800 points for a total of 1600 points. These are called the scaled scores, and are converted from the raw score, which is the number of questions answered correctly. Skipped or incorrect answers are not added or subtracted to the raw score. The College Board also equates each score, since tests on each test day slightly vary.
Essays have their own, separate scoring system from 2 to 8, with 8 being the highest. Two separate readers score the essay on a scale of 1-4 on categories called Reading, Analysis, and Essay. Then, they are added together for a maximum of 8 total points.
Viewing and sending scores can be done through the College Board website.
